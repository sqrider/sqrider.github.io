About me!

Greetings! I'm Young Jin, undergraduate Computer Engineering student at University of California, San Diego.

Computer Science interests me because it holds the key to understanding and creating the technology that our generation has grown up with. Computers and programs permeate every aspect of our lives, and shows up everywhere you look. Elementary school students learn how to type into word processors almost as soon as they learn to write with a pencil. Computers carefully monitor critical patients in hospitals and keep them alive. These are integrals parts of our lives that are made possible by advances in computer science.

From a young age, my biggest (guilty) pleasure has been gaming. Video games were all what my friends and I would talk about, and they've subsequently fostered my interest at what goes behind the scenes. I'm sure many children have said that they want to grow up to make video games, but for me the magic of computer science is that I can create anything I want. This website is a perfect example! It was something I threw together one night at LAHacks, April 2015. It's a thrilling thought that if I can set my mind to making something, it can be done. Websites are only the beginning, and I plan on applying my studies to all sorts of things.

For example, did you know that you can utilize the Doppler effect with the microphone and speakers on your laptop sense rudimentary motion? A Swedish CS student, Daniel Rapp, wrote a basic Chrome API that showcases that effect. When I discovered this, it blew my mind. I never thought that I could wave my hands in front of my screen like some sort of Star Trek character and have it affect it. If you're running Google Chrome, scroll down to Projects, and if it's finished, give it a try! (You'll have to enable your mic and speakers.)
